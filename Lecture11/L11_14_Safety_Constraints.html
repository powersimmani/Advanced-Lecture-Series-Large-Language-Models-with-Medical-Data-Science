Safety Constraints in Medical RLHF
Why Safety Constraints?
Medical AI requires hard constraints to prevent harmful outputs that could endanger patients, regardless of reward optimization.
Constraint Types
Hard Medical Rules: Never violate established clinical guidelines
Dosage Limits: Enforce safe medication dosing ranges
Contraindication Checking: Prevent dangerous drug interactions
Appropriate Scope: Stay within system's trained competency
Referral Triggers: Recommend specialist consultation when needed
Implementation Methods
Constrained Optimization: Add penalty terms for constraint violations
Rejection Sampling: Filter out unsafe outputs during generation
Rule-Based Filters: Apply deterministic safety checks
Multi-Objective RL: Balance reward with safety objectives
Monitoring & Enforcement
Real-time constraint checking during inference
Logging all constraint violations for review
Automatic escalation for high-risk situations
Regular safety audits by medical experts
