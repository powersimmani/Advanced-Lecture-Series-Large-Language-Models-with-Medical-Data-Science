KL Divergence Control
Purpose of KL Divergence
KL divergence measures how much the optimized policy deviates from the original base model, preventing harmful divergence from foundational knowledge.
Mathematical Formulation
KL(π_θ || π_ref) = E[log(π_θ(a|s)) - log(π_ref(a|s))]
π_θ: Current policy being optimized
π_ref: Reference policy (original base model)
Measures distribution difference between policies
Why KL Control Matters
Preserve Base Knowledge: Maintain general medical knowledge
Prevent Mode Collapse: Avoid generating repetitive outputs
Stability: Reduce training instability
Generalization: Better performance on out-of-distribution inputs
Tuning KL Penalty
High β: Strong constraint, minimal deviation from base model
Low β: More freedom to optimize, risk of forgetting
Adaptive β: Adjust penalty dynamically during training
Typical Range: β ∈ [0.01, 0.1] for medical applications
