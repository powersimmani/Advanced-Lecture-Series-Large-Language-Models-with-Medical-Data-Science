üìã Rare Genetics Case Study Overview
Approximately 7,000 rare genetic diseases are known worldwide, and while individual disease incidence rates are low, they collectively affect about 6-8% of the population, making them an important public health issue. This case study covers data science approaches for diagnosing and treating rare genetic diseases, and teaches methods for building effective analysis and predictive models in limited data environments.
üí° Real-World Application Examples
For certain rare genetic diseases, there may be only a few hundred patients worldwide. In such situations, traditional large-scale data-driven machine learning approaches are difficult to apply. Therefore, innovative approaches using transfer learning, meta-learning, and few-shot learning techniques are necessary.
üîç Research Methodology

1. Data Collection & Integration

Collect and integrate information from various sources (genomic data, clinical records, imaging data) to construct comprehensive patient profiles.

2. Feature Extraction & Selection

Identify key biomarkers and genetic variants related to diseases from high-dimensional genomic data and apply dimensionality reduction techniques.

3. Model Development & Validation

Use ensemble techniques and regularization methods suitable for small datasets to prevent overfitting and improve generalization performance.

4. Clinical Application & Evaluation

Validate developed models in actual clinical settings and evaluate performance using metrics such as sensitivity, specificity, and positive predictive value.

The use of AI and data science in the field of rare genetic diseases continues to expand. Through data sharing via multi-institutional collaborative networks, advances in synthetic data generation technology, and the introduction of privacy-preserving technologies such as federated learning, accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment will become available to more patients.
